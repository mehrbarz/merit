ON INTUITIVE KNOWLEDGE

  1. First, there is the kind which simply asserts the existence of the sense-datum, without in any way analysing it.
  2. The other kind arises when the object of sense is complex, and we subject it to some degree of analysis. If, for instance, we see a round patch of red, we may judge 'that patch of red is round'.
  3. Another example of this kind of judgement is 'this is to the right of that', where 'this' and 'that' are seen simultaneously.
